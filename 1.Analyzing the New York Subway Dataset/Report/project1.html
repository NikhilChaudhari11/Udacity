Section 1. Statistical Test

\(\qquad\)
Based on the QQ-plot above, we can see that the distribution of turnstile entries is not Gaussian normal. Thus, the statistical test that require underlying distribution assumption is not appropriate for this situation. Therefore, we should use Non-parametric Test for testing whether there is significant difference of turnstile entries between rainy day and not-rainy day.

Based on the result of Mann-Whitney U test, we can see there is significant difference of turnstile entries between rainy day and not-rainy day. When its rainy, there will be more people willing to take the subway.

A1: Mann-Whitney U test. two-tail P value, because we dont have prior knowledge about which one will have bigger value. the null hypothesis is the distributions of rainy-day turnstile entries and not-rainy-day turnstile entries differ by a location shift of 0.

A2: Because the distribution of turnstile entries is definitely not Gaussian normal distribution. but distributions of two samples are similar to each other. Thus, Mann-Whitney U test are applicable and appropriate.

A3: From the Mann-Whitney U test, the p-value is 5.482e-06. The mean of rainy-day turnstile entries is about 2028. The mean of not-rainy-day turnstile entries is about 1846.

A4: In rainy day, there are more people willing to take subway. And the rainy or not-rainy is informative to the prediction of turnstile entries.

Section 2. Linear Regression

A1: Ordinary least squares

A2:
We use \(log \left(1+x\right)\) transformation to approximate normal distribution.
Categorical Variable: weekday, fog, rain, hour.
Numeric Variable about Weather: precipi, pressurei, tempi, wspdi, meanprecipi, meanpressurei, meantempi, meanwspdi.
Numeric Variable about Entries: Lagged value of turnstile entries (lagged period from 1 to 6).
NOTE: To Compute a lagged version of a time series, we just shift the time base back by a given number of observations. Lagged Explanatory Variables are lagged values \(x_{t-k}\) of observed variables \(x_{t}\).

A3:
Categorical Variables are about weather and time. we think fog and rain are informative for prediction. Plus, we bring weekday and hour in because we think that the mean value of turnstile entries is time-variant. All Numeric Variables about Weather conditions may have influence on turnstile entries. Even though, put all these variables into the model may bring multicollinearity problem, we want to see the variables explaining potential first, and then we may do feature selection. At last, we also think that yesterdays turnstile entries can help us predict todays turnstile entries. In statistics perspective, we should solve this problem using time series regression and use arima model with seasonal difference of period 6. However, we can also solve this problem in a machine learning perspective, therefore we put lagged series in our linear regression model as features.
For data exploration perspective: If we only put all Categorical Variable and Numeric Variable about Weather into our model, we can only get an OLS model with \(R^{2}\) about 16%. if we put station as a dummy variable into our model, we can get an OLS model with \(R^{2}\) about 49%, this result have a big improvement, because now we think that the mean value for each station are different. Still, if we put lagged series of turnstile entries (lagged period from 1 to 6) into our model, we can get an OLS model with \(R^{2}\) about 75%. the lagged period goes to 6 because our datas frequency is 6 times per day.

Section 5. Reflection

A1:
There are some potential shortcomings of the data set and our method.
shortcomings of the data set:
First, the data set is relatively small. More data points may bring us better result, because we actually run this regression model for each station.
Second, there may be some potential error of in the data set. Some of turnstile entries are 0. These stations in that period may be closed. Thus, if we put those data points into our modeling, it may ruin our model in some degree.
Last, we may need more variable, such as some traffic statistics on the ground: degree of traffic jam , number of people on the bus, todays number of people entering into NYC through New York Penn and Port Authority etc.
shortcomings of our method:
Since the distribution of training data set are not Gaussian normal. And we have heteroscedasticity and multicollinearity problem. we may use some robust regression method.
Plus, we didnt do any feature engineering, we can try polynomial regression or use generating more linear features based on other variable in the data set. Or, we can also use some regression method that can learn pattern from residuals.
